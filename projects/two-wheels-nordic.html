Two Wheels Nordic

A community forum platform built for Nordic motorcycle enthusiasts. Riders can create accounts, join discussions across organized topic categories, and connect with other motorcyclists across the region — all under the tagline "Where Riders Connect."

Overview

Two Wheels Nordic is a community-driven forum platform tailored to motorcycle riders across the Nordic countries. The goal was to create a focused, clutter-free space where riders could discuss everything from routes and gear to maintenance and events — without the noise of a generic social network.

The platform features organised discussion categories, user accounts, and a clean reading experience optimised for both desktop and mobile. Privacy is kept simple: only essential cookies are used, with no third-party tracking.

Key Features

  • Categorised discussion forums covering routes, gear, maintenance, and events
  • User registration and authentication with secure login
  • Thread-based discussions with reply support
  • Minimal, distraction-free UI focused on readability
  • Privacy-first cookie policy — essential cookies only
  • Responsive layout optimised for mobile and desktop

Design Approach

The design prioritises function over flash. Navigation is straightforward, forum categories are visually distinct, and the reading experience stays clean on all screen sizes. The understated visual style reflects the Nordic aesthetic — simple, purposeful, and built to last.

What I Learned

Building a community platform meant thinking beyond the UI — user flows for registration, moderation considerations, and making sure the forum hierarchy felt intuitive at a glance. The project sharpened my approach to information architecture and reinforced the value of keeping a product focused on its core use case.
